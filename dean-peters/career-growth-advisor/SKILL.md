## Purpose

Guide product leaders through the specific challenges of career transitions using adaptive coaching. This skill covers the full leadership progression — from first-time Director to seasoned CPO — as three sequential phases, each with distinct challenges, frameworks, and failure modes.

This is not generic leadership advice. Each phase names what's actually hard, why it's hard, and what to do about it.

## Which Phase Do You Need?

| Your situation | Phase | Reference |
|---|---|---|
| PM thinking about or preparing for Director | **Phase 1: PM → Director** | `references/phase-1-pm-to-director.md` |
| Director evaluating or starting VP/CPO | **Phase 2: Director → VP/CPO** | `references/phase-2-director-to-vp-cpo.md` |
| New VP/CPO in first 90 days | **Phase 3: Executive Onboarding** | `references/phase-3-executive-onboarding.md` |

## How to Use

1. **Identify the user's situation** — Ask: "Where are you in your career journey?" Use the table above to route.
2. **Load the relevant phase** — Read the reference file for that phase.
3. **Follow the phase's coaching protocol** — Each phase has its own diagnostic questions, frameworks, and coaching flow.
4. **Cross-reference when needed** — A Director preparing for VP should start with Phase 2, but may benefit from Phase 3's onboarding protocol once they land the role.

## Phase Summaries

### Phase 1: PM → Director
The fundamental shift from **doing the work** to **enabling the work**. Key frameworks:
- Altitude & Horizon model (scope × time)
- The Waiter-to-Operator shift
- Four transition zones and their failure modes
- See: `references/phase-1-pm-to-director.md`

### Phase 2: Director → VP/CPO
The shift from **leading a team** to **leading an organization**. Key frameworks:
- The Three Ps (Product, Practice, People)
- Unwritten strategy and the empowerment myth
- Alliance building and executive peer relationships
- See: `references/phase-2-director-to-vp-cpo.md`

### Phase 3: Executive Onboarding (First 90 Days)
Structure the transition as a **diagnostic process, not an execution sprint**. Key frameworks:
- Diagnose (Month 1) → Validate (Month 2) → Act with Evidence (Month 3)
- The Consultant Mindset
- Surfacing unwritten strategy and assessing inherited teams
- See: `references/phase-3-executive-onboarding.md`

## Common Failure Modes Across All Phases

1. **Acting before understanding** — The #1 failure at every level. Each phase has a diagnostic-first protocol.
2. **Hero syndrome** — Doing IC work because it feels productive. Especially dangerous in Phase 1.
3. **Strategy by assertion** — Declaring strategy without the evidence base. Phase 3 specifically guards against this.
4. **Ignoring the political landscape** — Relationships matter more at each successive level.
