## When to use this skill

To write internal communications:
- 3P updates (Progress, Plans, Problems)
- Company newsletters
- FAQ responses
- Status reports
- Leadership updates
- Project updates
- Incident reports

## How to use this skill

1. **Identify the communication type** from the request
2. **Apply the appropriate format**:

### 3P Update (Progress, Plans, Problems)
```markdown
## [Team/Project] — 3P Update ([Date])

### Progress (what we shipped/completed)
- [Accomplishment with impact]

### Plans (what's next)
- [Planned work with timeline]

### Problems (blockers, risks, asks)
- [Issue with context and ask]
```

### Company Newsletter
- Lead with biggest news/win
- Keep sections scannable (bullets, not paragraphs)
- Include a people/culture section
- End with what's ahead

### FAQ Response
- Lead with the direct answer
- Follow with context/reasoning
- Anticipate the follow-up question
- Keep under 150 words per answer

### General Internal Comms
- Know your audience (all-hands vs. team vs. leadership)
- Lead with the "so what"
- Be specific about asks and deadlines
- Use plain language, not corporate-speak

## Tone Guidelines

- **Clear over clever** — Say what you mean
- **Concise** — Respect people's time
- **Action-oriented** — Every update should answer "so what?"
- **Honest** — Bad news delivered early builds trust
