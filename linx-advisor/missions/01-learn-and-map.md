# Mission: Phase 1 — Learn & Map (Days 1-30)

## Goal
Understand the terrain. Earn the right to have opinions. Build relationships before shipping artifacts.

## Timeline
- Start: April 12, 2026
- End: May 12, 2026

## Success Criteria
- [ ] 5+ customer conversations (direct or observed)
- [ ] Stakeholder map populated with real observations (not assumptions)
- [ ] Product architecture understood — can sketch system diagram unprompted
- [ ] Competitive landscape validated with 3+ internal stakeholders
- [ ] First artifact shipped + shared with sales/CS + feedback received
- [ ] 1 "aha" insight logged — something nobody else was tracking

## Key Activities

### Week 1 (Apr 12-18): Listen Mode
- Meet every team lead. Ask: "What's working? What's broken? What do you wish the PM team did differently?"
- Sit in on customer calls (don't talk, just listen). Log signals in log.md.
- 1:1 with Niv: understand his product vision, what he delegates vs. owns, communication style.
- 1:1 with Israel: Series B timeline, what he needs from product, competitive positioning in deals.
- DO NOT share competitive analysis yet. Listen first.

### Week 2 (Apr 19-25): Map the System
- Get access to all product systems (Jira/Linear, analytics, support tickets, CRM).
- Map the product architecture with engineering. Ask for a whiteboard session.
- Identify the top 3 customer pain points from support/CS data.
- Continue customer call observation. Target: 2+ calls this week.

### Week 3 (Apr 26 — May 2): Validate & Share
- Share competitive positioning with Niv + 1-2 sales reps. Get feedback.
- Validate battle cards against real deal objections. Are they accurate?
- Start building the governance V1.1 understanding — what's being built, what's blocked, what's the timeline?
- Log first "aha" insight.

### Week 4 (May 3-12): First Artifact
- Ship one thing: competitive positioning one-pager, updated battle card, or customer insight synthesis.
- Share it. Get feedback. Iterate.
- Phase 1 review: run through milestones. What did you learn? What surprised you?

## Anti-Patterns to Avoid
- Strategy docs before customer conversations
- Challenging founders in week 1 (earn trust first)
- Over-indexing on competitive analysis when customer data is available
- Trying to own everything at once — pick one domain to go deep on

- `career-growth-advisor` — Phase 3 (Executive Onboarding) protocol maps well to this
- `stakeholder-buyin` — for navigating Niv/Israel relationship building
