# Mission: Pre-Onboarding (March 29 — April 11)

## Goal
Arrive at Linx on April 12 as the most prepared new PM possible. Validate the advisor system, build the daily log habit, and internalize competitive positioning.

## Timeline
- Start: March 29, 2026
- End: April 11, 2026 (day before start)

## Success Criteria
- [ ] Daily log habit tested: logged something 8+ out of 14 days
- [ ] Morning brief + Friday scorecard cycle run for 2 full weeks
- [ ] System feedback: documented what works, what's noise, what to adjust
- [ ] Can name all 8 competitors, their scores, and Linx's positioning cold
- [ ] Read all 4 Linx onboarding PDFs (agent delegation, IAM for LLMs, Gartner market guide, IAM attack surface)
- [ ] "First week questions" list prepared for Niv and Israel
- [ ] Competitive positioning one-pager drafted (first artifact candidate for Phase 1)

## Key Activities

### Week 1 (Mar 29 — Apr 4)
- Read onboarding PDFs. Log key takeaways in log.md.
- Review competitive matrix + battle cards. Challenge any scores that feel wrong.
- Draft first-week questions for Niv (product vision, roadmap priorities, governance V1.1 status, customer feedback patterns) and Israel (Series B timeline, ARR targets, hiring plan, competitive positioning in deals).
- Test daily brief cycle — does it generate useful content?

### Week 2 (Apr 5 — Apr 11)
- Draft competitive positioning one-pager using positioning.md as source.
- Practice the "only we" pitch out loud. Can you explain Linx's differentiation in 30 seconds?
- Review milestones.md Phase 1 criteria — are they realistic? Adjust if needed.
- System review: what did the morning briefs get right? What was missing? Any scheduled task output that was noise?
- Final prep: calendar setup, workspace ready, tools configured.

## Risks
- Over-preparing on competitive data, under-preparing on relationship strategy
- Building artifacts before understanding internal context
- The system might produce low-value briefs since there's no calendar data yet — that's expected
