# Example: Meeting Prep

**Omri:** "I have a call tomorrow with the CISO at a financial services company evaluating us against ConductorOne."

**Advisor response:**

Here's your prep. 3 minutes to read.

**ConductorOne battle card (key points):**
- Their strengths: hours-to-deploy, 300+ connectors, 400% YoY growth, strong tech logos
- Their weakness in this deal: NHI/agentic governance is newly announced (Feb 2025), unproven. No SOC2 Type II publicly confirmed — big gap for financial services.
- Financial services angle: they lack fine-grained SoD controls and SAP integration depth

**Your 3 angles for this call:**

1. **"Machine identities outnumber humans 17:1 in financial services."** ConductorOne started with human access reviews. Their NHI is 6 months old. Ours is architecturally native — one graph, not a bolt-on.

2. **"Autonomous remediation with guardrails."** Financial services CISOs are paranoid about "autonomous" — lead with the guardrails: Confidence × Risk Matrix, progressive autonomy, kill switch, full audit trail. This is your assignment work — use it.

3. **"Compliance readiness."** Honest gap: our compliance reporting is on the roadmap. BUT — our continuous monitoring catches risks ConductorOne's periodic reviews miss. Frame it as: "real-time compliance posture vs. quarterly checkbox exercise."

**3 likely objections:**

1. *"ConductorOne has 300+ connectors, how many do you have?"* — Don't answer with a number if it's low. Say: "We prioritize depth over breadth. For your environment, which apps are critical? Let's validate connector coverage for your specific stack."

2. *"You're Series A, ConductorOne raised $111M"* — "Our investors include Index Ventures, Cyberstarts, and Wiz founders. We're smaller and more focused — which means your deployment gets engineering attention, not just a sales team."

3. *"Show me a financial services reference"* — If you don't have one, be honest. "We're building our financial services practice. Our approach to compliance-grade identity governance is informed by [Omri's Payoneer/Cedar Money background]. We can share our compliance roadmap."

**2 discovery questions to ask:**
- "What percentage of your identities are non-human today? How do you govern them?"
- "When was your last access certification campaign? How confident are you in the results?"

**Jargon bridge:** SoD = Segregation of Duties = maker-checker in payments. Same concept, different domain.
