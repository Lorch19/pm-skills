# Battle Cards — Tier 1 Competitors
*Last updated: 2026-03-29 | For internal + sales use*

---

## SailPoint (89/100) — Incumbent Leader

**Their pitch:** "The market-leading identity security platform with the deepest governance, broadest integrations, and proven enterprise scale."

**Reality check:** Deployment takes months, NHI is bolt-on, AI is recommendation-tier (not autonomous), and their SaaS migration (Atlas) is still in progress for many customers.

| Strengths | Weaknesses |
|---|---|
| 1000+ connectors — widest ecosystem | Deployment: 4-6+ months to production |
| Deepest governance (JML, SoD, role mining) | No autonomous remediation — still recommendation-tier AI |
| Gartner MQ Leader 6 years running | NHI is newer addition, not architecturally native |
| $1.125B ARR, re-IPO'd | Premium pricing, complex licensing |
| FedRAMP, SOC2, ISO — full cert stack | Legacy on-prem (IdentityIQ) customers still migrating to Atlas |

**When Linx wins:** Customer values speed-to-value, needs NHI/agentic governance, wants AI-driven autonomous action, is not locked into SailPoint already.

**When Linx loses:** Customer needs deep IGA governance (JML, SoD, compliance reporting), has 500+ apps to integrate, requires FedRAMP, or procurement mandates Gartner MQ leadership.

**Talk track:**
- "SailPoint is great if you have 12 months and need to govern human access to hundreds of apps. But identities have changed — machine identities outnumber humans 17:1, AI agents are making autonomous decisions, and risk accumulates 24/7 while SailPoint runs quarterly reviews."
- "We give you visibility across ALL identity types in days, not months. And we act on risks autonomously, not just flag them for a human to review next quarter."

**Trap to avoid:** Don't position as "replacing SailPoint." Position as "solving what SailPoint can't" — NHI, agentic, autonomous remediation, speed.

## CyberArk (83.5/100) — PAM-to-IGA Leader

**Their pitch:** "The identity security platform — PAM, IGA, machine identity, workforce identity, all unified."

**Reality check:** "Unified" via 3 acquisitions (Zilla, Venafi, workforce identity) that are still being integrated. Each product has different architectures, UIs, and deployment models.

| Strengths | Weaknesses |
|---|---|
| PAM market dominance (~30% share) | IGA via Zilla acquisition — integration is early |
| 55% F500 penetration | Machine identity via Venafi — separate product line |
| $1.44B ARR, ~$25B PANW acquisition | Multiple acquired architectures, not unified |
| Massive integration ecosystem (800+ C3) | Opaque, premium pricing + heavy services |
| FedRAMP High certification | Complexity — full platform deployment is months |

**When Linx wins:** Customer sees through the "unified platform" M&A story, wants native cross-identity coverage, doesn't need PAM, values speed and AI-native over breadth.

**When Linx loses:** Customer is already a CyberArk PAM customer (strong lock-in), needs privileged access management, or is buying a bundled deal with PANW.

**Talk track:**
- "CyberArk is assembling a platform through acquisitions — Zilla for IGA, Venafi for machine identity. That means three different architectures, three different data models, and a long integration timeline. We built one platform from day one — one graph, one AI engine, all identity types native."
- "Ask them: can their AI reason across human access AND service accounts AND AI agents in a single decision? We can."

**Trap to avoid:** Don't attack CyberArk's PAM — it's excellent. Attack the M&A integration gap and the lack of AI-native architecture.

## ConductorOne (81/100) — Modern Challenger

**Their pitch:** "Modern identity governance — deploy in hours, not months. Unified human and NHI governance."

**Reality check:** Very strong execution but NHI/agentic coverage is nascent (announced Feb 2025), and AI is workflow-centric, not truly autonomous across identity types.

| Strengths | Weaknesses |
|---|---|
| Hours-to-weeks deployment | NHI/agentic governance newly announced, unproven |
| 300+ connectors, growing fast | Primarily workflow-tier AI, not autonomous remediation |
| $111M raised, 400% YoY revenue growth | ~102 employees — execution capacity limits |
| Strong tech logos (DoorDash, Instacart) | No SOC2 Type II / ISO 27001 publicly confirmed |
| CrowdStrike as strategic investor | Missing fine-grained SoD, SAP controls |

**When Linx wins:** Customer needs NHI + agentic identity governance (not just human IGA), wants autonomous remediation (not just workflow automation), values graph-based cross-identity reasoning.

**When Linx loses:** Customer's primary need is human IGA (access reviews, lifecycle), needs 300+ connectors, or is swayed by ConductorOne's revenue growth and funding.

**Talk track:**
- "ConductorOne is fast and modern — we respect that. But they started with human access reviews and are adding NHI. We started with a unified identity graph — human, NHI, and agentic from day one. That architectural difference means we see risks they can't: an AI agent chaining access through a service account to reach a human's permissions."
- "Their AI automates workflows. Our AI reasons across identity types and remediates autonomously."

**Trap to avoid:** Don't dismiss ConductorOne — they're well-funded and fast. Focus on architectural differentiation (graph depth) and identity breadth.

## Lumos (80/100) — AI-Native Challenger

**Their pitch:** "AI-first identity governance. Albus — the first multi-agent AI system for autonomous identity security."

**Reality check:** Best AI in the category, but Gartner confirms they do NOT extend to certificate/API-key governance for NHI. They're autonomous for human identity — not for the full identity landscape.

| Strengths | Weaknesses |
|---|---|
| Albus: genuinely autonomous multi-agent AI | No NHI certificate/API-key governance (Gartner) |
| 300+ integrations, fast deployment | Primarily SaaS-focused, limited on-prem |
| Strong tech logos (Pinterest, MongoDB) | No agentic AI identity governance |
| Identity Security Agents (March 2026) | $65M total funding — less runway than ConductorOne |
| Best AI marketing narrative in category | Mid-market focused, limited F500 enterprise |

**When Linx wins:** Customer needs NHI governance (service accounts, API keys, certificates), needs agentic AI identity governance, operates hybrid/on-prem environments, wants cross-identity risk reasoning.

**When Linx loses:** Customer's primary need is human SaaS governance, prioritizes AI autonomy over identity breadth, or is swayed by Lumos's stronger AI marketing narrative.

**Talk track:**
- "Lumos has excellent AI for human identity governance. But here's the gap: Gartner notes they don't cover certificate or API-key governance for machine identities. Machine identities outnumber humans 17:1. AI agents are the fastest-growing identity type. Lumos's AI is blind to the majority of your identity surface."
- "We cover all identity types — human, NHI, and agentic — with one AI engine. That's not just broader. It's structurally more accurate, because identity risks cross boundaries."

**Trap to avoid:** Don't compete on AI narrative head-to-head — Lumos has better AI marketing. Compete on identity coverage breadth where they have a Gartner-confirmed gap.
